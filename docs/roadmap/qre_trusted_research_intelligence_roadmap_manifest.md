# QRE Trusted Research Intelligence Roadmap Manifest

## Status

Active supporting roadmap manifest for the campaign-first trusted research
intelligence program selected by `ADE-QRE-016H`.

This document governs the `ADE-QRE-017` program and its child queue items.
It supplements current canonical roadmap material without replacing historical
Roadmap v6, Addendum, or prior ADE/QRE records.

## Program thesis

The largest maturity gap is not "more modules."

The repository already contains many scaffolds and several working read-only
capabilities. The required transition is:

`scaffold -> populated working capability -> real evidence density ->
repeatable research decisions -> operator-trusted research intelligence`

New implementation is justified only when it:

- populates existing trusted-loop evidence;
- integrates existing components already present in the repository;
- repairs a demonstrated blocker;
- improves repeatability;
- improves decision usefulness;
- improves operator trust; or
- directly enables or evaluates the broad preregistered campaign.

## Current baseline

- queue governance, status audit, and post-merge evidence discipline exist;
- trusted-loop summaries, reason-record audits, routing/sampling readiness,

- research memory, funnel diagnostics, source lifecycle, cache manifests,
  historical accounting, contradiction/staleness, and controlled validation
  scaffolds exist;

  insufficient campaign-scale closure;

  bounded generation authority must be admitted by a separate governed program.

## Target state

The program is complete only when QRE can support operator-trusted,
campaign-driven research decisions with:

- explicit behavior theses;
- quality-gated and identity-aware data readiness;
- complete or explicitly unavailable KPI states;
- non-empty evidence-linked reason records when evidence exists;
- routing and sampling decisions proven against deterministic baselines;
- durable failure memory and contradiction visibility;
- broad preregistered campaign execution with complete funnel accounting;
- repeated independent OOS evidence where data permits; and
- a final synthesis-readiness review that keeps synthesis blocked unless all
  prerequisites are met.

## Strategic workstreams

### A. Research Loop Maturity and Evidence Density

Populate and reconcile trusted-loop evidence so operator-facing claims derive
from real repository evidence rather than scaffolds.

### B. Screening Funnel Calibration

Make the funnel measurable, diagnosis-ready, and recommendation-bounded before
any criteria move.

### C. Quality-Gated Research Data Supply Chain

Reuse existing data, source lifecycle, and cache surfaces to establish a
versioned, quality-gated input foundation.

### D. Behavior Thesis Engine

Replace ad hoc signal expansion with deterministic, falsifiable behavior theses
and evidence attachment.

### E. Actionable Failure Intelligence

Turn failures into bounded, evidence-backed next actions and usefulness
tracking.

### F. Routing and Sampling Calibration

Prove routing and sampling usefulness against simple deterministic baselines.

### G. Epistemological Research Memory

Keep prior failures, contradictions, lineage, and evidence decay visible and
retrievable without treating retrieval as truth.

### H. Operator-Trusted Research Observability

Produce concise, evidence-linked decision outputs that support repeatable
operator review.

### I. Broad Preregistered Campaign

Freeze hypotheses, data identities, windows, criteria, and controls before
execution.

### J. Controlled Learning and Replay

Allow at most one justified criterion-class recalibration, exact replay, and
independent OOS repetition.

## Phase sequence

### Phase 0 - Baseline Reconciliation

Reconcile existing capabilities, maturity states, blockers, and evidence gaps.

### Phase 1 - Evidence Population

Populate evidence classes, reason records, readiness states, and KPI snapshots.

### Phase 2 - Failure and Funnel Understanding

Measure funnel losses, threshold distances, failure attribution, and action
usefulness.

### Phase 3 - Data Readiness Unblock

Mature OHLCV/cache, source quality, PIT, identity, and source usefulness
surfaces.

### Phase 4 - Behavior Thesis Maturity

Establish deterministic behavior-thesis, supporting evidence, contradiction, and
opportunity-value surfaces.

### Phase 5 - Routing and Sampling Proof

Compare routing, sampling, and suppression against deterministic baselines.

### Phase 6 - Research Memory Maturity

Make lineage and evidence decay explicit and operator-inspectable.

### Phase 7 - Operator Trust

Consolidate one-page decision reports and why-explored / why-failed /
why-blocked surfaces.

### Phase 8 - Broad Campaign Execution

Freeze and execute the preregistered campaign with full funnel accounting.

### Phase 9 - Controlled Recalibration and Replay

Allow one evidence-justified criterion-class change, replay, and repeated
independent OOS when possible.

### Phase 10 - Synthesis Readiness Review

Produce a review-only final outcome:

- `CONTINUE_BLOCKED`
- `ELIGIBLE_FOR_SEPARATE_SYNTHESIS_DESIGN_REVIEW`
- `INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE`

## Promotion and gating rules

- No child item becomes executable merely because it exists in the queue.
- A queue item may become `ready` only when documented prerequisites are done.
- Real-data and campaign items retain explicit evidence and data gates.
- `ADE-QRE-017Y`, `ADE-QRE-017AA`, `ADE-QRE-017AB`, and `ADE-QRE-017AC`
  require documented campaign inputs and outputs, not scaffold placeholders.
- `ADE-QRE-017AD` cannot conclude synthesis eligibility unless evidence density,
  reason records, routing usefulness, sampling usefulness, data quality,
  reproducibility, contradiction visibility, null controls, and repeated
  independent OOS are all satisfied where required.

## KPIs

The program tracks at minimum:

- capability counts by maturity class;
- evidence class population completeness;
- reason-record non-empty rate when evidence exists;
- routing-ready and sampling-ready counts;
- KPI completeness and historical snapshot continuity;
- funnel stage counts and threshold-distance coverage;
- failure attribution coverage and insufficient-evidence rate;
- action usefulness follow-through;
- source quality failures, usefulness, and cache hit rate;
- thesis support / contradiction / unresolved counts;
- routing and sampling baseline deltas;
- duplicate/dead-zone suppression efficacy;
- campaign completion, rejection, blocked, timeout, and error accounting;
- accepted OOS and repeated independent OOS coverage; and
- operator decision report completeness.

## Explicit deferred scope

Deferred or forbidden for this program:

- strategy synthesis implementation inside ADE-QRE-017;
- executable strategy generation inside ADE-QRE-017;
- live, paper, or shadow activation;
- broker, risk, execution, or capital-allocation changes;
- hidden selection systems, stochastic mutation, or genetic programming;
- indicator-family expansion without an explicit behavior thesis;
- mutation of frozen public contracts;
- `.claude/**` edits;
- direct edits to generated projections.

## Definition of Done

The full `ADE-QRE-017` program is done only when:

- `ADE-QRE-017A` through `ADE-QRE-017AD` are merged and post-merge validated;
- queue state, tests, and projections remain consistent;
- the broad preregistered campaign has complete funnel accounting;
- repeated independent OOS evidence is either produced or blocked with precise
  evidence-backed reasons;
- final operator decision outputs are complete; and
- `ADE-QRE-017AD` records a review-only synthesis-readiness outcome without
  implementing synthesis.
